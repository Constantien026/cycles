The Nitrogen Cycle

How Nitrogen Moves Through Air, Soil, Plants, Animals & Bacteria

⚡
01
Nitrogen Fixation
Nitrogen-fixing bacteria (Rhizobium, Azotobacter) and lightning convert inert atmospheric N₂ into ammonia (NH₃) or ammonium (NH₄⁺) that organisms can use.
🔄
02
Nitrification
Nitrifying bacteria (Nitrosomonas, Nitrobacter) convert ammonium (NH₄⁺) step-by-step into nitrite (NO₂⁻) and then nitrate (NO₃⁻) that plants can absorb.
🌱
03
Assimilation
Plants absorb nitrate (NO₃⁻) and ammonium through their roots, incorporating nitrogen into amino acids, proteins, chlorophyll, and nucleic acids (DNA/RNA).
💀
04
Ammonification
Decomposing bacteria and fungi break down nitrogen-containing compounds in dead organisms and animal waste, releasing ammonia (NH₃) back into the soil.
💨
05
Denitrification
Anaerobic denitrifying bacteria (Pseudomonas) in waterlogged soils convert nitrates (NO₃⁻) back into N₂ or N₂O gas, completing the cycle by returning nitrogen to the atmosphere.
🦠
06
Bacteria — The Key Players
Unlike the water and carbon cycles, the nitrogen cycle is almost entirely driven by microorganisms. Without these bacteria, nitrogen would remain locked as unusable N₂ in the atmosphere.
