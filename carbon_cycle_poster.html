The Carbon Cycle

How Carbon Flows Through Living Organisms, Atmosphere & Earth

🌿
01
Photosynthesis
Plants, algae, and cyanobacteria absorb CO₂ from the atmosphere and convert it into glucose using sunlight energy, releasing oxygen as a byproduct.
🫁
02
Respiration
All living organisms break down glucose for energy, releasing CO₂ and water back into the atmosphere as metabolic waste.
🦠
03
Decomposition
Bacteria and fungi break down dead organisms and organic waste, recycling carbon compounds and releasing CO₂ or methane back into the environment.
🔥
04
Combustion
Burning fossil fuels (coal, oil, gas) and biomass rapidly oxidizes carbon compounds, releasing large amounts of CO₂ into the atmosphere.
🌫️
05
CO₂ in Atmosphere
Carbon dioxide accumulates as a greenhouse gas in the atmosphere, trapping heat energy and regulating Earth's temperature. Current levels exceed 420 ppm.
🌊
06
Ocean Uptake
Oceans absorb approximately 30% of human-produced CO₂, dissolving it in seawater and sequestering carbon in marine organisms and sediments.
